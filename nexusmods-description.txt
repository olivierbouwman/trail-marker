[size=4][b]Description[/b][/size]
Trail Marker quietly records where you go in Red Dead Redemption 2 while you play, then lets you see your whole journey drawn on the map.
It runs in the background and logs your position and a little context (about once a second) to a tiny file. No menus, no hotkeys, nothing to configure — install it once and forget it's there. Later, drop your log onto the web viewer and view or replay your whole journey on the map: how you traveled, where you fought and died, your honor rising and falling, and your wallet over time. When you're done, export it as a print-ready poster or a share-ready image of your playthrough.
[b]Web viewer (no install):[/b] [url=https://olivierbouwman.github.io/trail-marker/]olivierbouwman.github.io/trail-marker[/url] — your data is read entirely in your browser, nothing is ever uploaded.

[size=4][b]Installation instructions[/b][/size]
[list=1]
[*]Install [b]ScriptHookRDR2 V2[/b] (download from its official source — not bundled here): its loader (e.g. dinput8.dll) and ScriptHookRDR2.dll.[/*]
[*]Download from the Files tab and extract [b]TrailMarker.asi[/b] from the archive, then copy it into your RDR2 folder (the one with RDR2.exe), alongside ScriptHook. (Mod managers like Vortex handle the extraction for you.)[/*]
[*]Launch the game and play. Your log is written to [b]TrailMarker.bin[/b] in that same folder.[/*]
[/list]
To view it, open the web viewer above and drag TrailMarker.bin onto the page. The hosted viewer is always the latest version; if you'd rather run it offline, grab a local copy from the GitHub repo. To stop logging, just remove TrailMarker.asi.

[size=4][b]Main features[/b][/size]
[list]
[*][b]Set-and-forget logging[/b] — runs in the background, no menus or hotkeys, nothing to configure.[/*]
[*][b]Smart sampling[/b] — records a point when you've actually moved, plus an occasional point while you're still, so travel is captured densely and idle time stays tiny.[/*]
[*][b]Transport mode[/b] for every point: on foot, horse, boat, train, balloon, swimming, or other vehicles.[/*]
[*][b]Context[/b] — in combat, wanted by the law, deaths, cutscenes, story and stranger missions, plus your honor, character (Arthur / John), and cash.[/*]
[*][b]Save-aware[/b] — marks session boundaries so the viewer can separate save branches and pinpoint where you died.[/*]
[*][b]Tiny and safe[/b] — a compact binary log (~65 MB even after 1000 hours), flushed continuously so a crash costs almost nothing.[/*]
[*][b]Web viewer[/b] — see your trails on the RDR2 map, colored by transport, honor, money, or combat, with death markers and filters (cutscenes, mission travel, fast-travel jumps, abandoned save branches).[/*]
[*][b]Replay your journey[/b] — play, pause, and scrub through your whole playthrough on the map, watching the trail draw itself in order with a running tally of miles ridden, in-game time, and deaths.[/*]
[*][b]A keepsake title block[/b] — the viewer composes an engraved-style map cartouche from your file, drawn with sketches from Arthur's own journal: "THE TRAIL OF ARTHUR MORGAN", the miles you rode, the years it spanned, and how your honor arc ended.[/*]
[*][b]Poster and share-image exports[/b] — render your map, trail, and cartouche as a clean print-ready 36×24 in poster (300 DPI) or a smaller image sized for social posts, with no UI chrome.[/*]
[/list]

[size=4][b]Requirements[/b][/size]
[list]
[*]Red Dead Redemption 2 (PC, [b]Story Mode only[/b] — do not use mods in RDR Online).[/*]
[*][b]ScriptHookRDR2 V2[/b], matching your game version. Tested on RDR2 build [b]1491.50[/b].[/*]
[/list]
[i]Heads-up:[/i] some antivirus flags .asi/ScriptHook plugins as a false positive. Trail Marker is open source — build it yourself if you'd rather. Use at your own risk.
